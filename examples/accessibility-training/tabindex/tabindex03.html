Shakespeare's Sonnets

The general subject is continued from Sonnets 1 and 2, with the poet presenting a new argument as to why his young friend should start a family. The young man need only look at his own mother to see how important his youth and beauty are to her, as a constant reminder of "the lovely April of her prime".
